# Dave Gerhardt

## Newsletter 1

### Title
Timeless B2B Marketing Lessons

### Published
June 23, 2026

### Author
Dave Gerhardt

### Source
Exit Five Newsletter

### Why Selected

This newsletter explores how exceptional writing, storytelling, and customer research create competitive advantages in an AI-driven marketing environment. The lessons directly apply to LinkedIn organic content because successful LinkedIn posts rely on authentic storytelling, audience understanding, and differentiated communication rather than generic AI-generated copy.

---

### Summary

Dave reflects on a conversation with copywriter Eddie Shleyner, arguing that while AI has dramatically improved research efficiency, human creativity remains the defining factor behind memorable marketing.

Using an experiment comparing AI-generated writing with human storytelling, the newsletter demonstrates that effective copy evokes emotion through observation, dialogue, and context rather than explicitly describing feelings.

Dave also explains that AI should primarily support research and preparation, allowing marketers to dedicate more time to strategic thinking and creative execution.

---

### Key Research Insights

#### Great writing shows instead of tells

Strong marketing copy allows readers to experience emotions naturally rather than explicitly describing them.

For LinkedIn, authentic stories outperform overly promotional messaging.

---

#### Great content begins with research

Customer interviews, reviews, forums, and conversations provide the language that creates persuasive marketing.

AI accelerates research but should not replace human interpretation.

---

#### AI should support creativity

Dave recommends using AI for:

- Research
- Summarization
- Brainstorming
- Organization

Human marketers remain responsible for originality, positioning, storytelling, and judgment.

---

#### Authentic storytelling creates differentiation

As AI-generated content becomes increasingly common, marketers who share personal experiences, observations, and unique perspectives become more memorable.

---

### Why It Matters

This newsletter explains why successful LinkedIn content is driven by thoughtful research, authentic storytelling, and human creativity rather than automation alone.

These principles support sustainable thought leadership for B2B SaaS marketers.

---

### Personal Research Notes

One recurring theme across Dave's content is that AI improves efficiency but cannot replace strategic thinking. Successful LinkedIn content should combine customer research with authentic experiences to create content that feels credible, differentiated, and genuinely useful.

# Dave Gerhardt

## Newsletter 2

### Title

How to Make Your B2B Influencer Marketing Work

### Published

June 16, 2026

### Author

Dave Gerhardt

### Source

Exit Five Newsletter

### Why Selected

This newsletter explains how influencer marketing functions as a brand-building strategy rather than a short-term demand generation tactic. Many of the principles discussed—including creator partnerships, trust, content repurposing, and audience credibility—translate directly into effective LinkedIn organic content strategies.

---

### Summary

Dave challenges the assumption that influencer marketing is only suitable for enterprise companies with large budgets.

Drawing insights from a conversation with Ishveen Jolly (CEO of OpenSponsorship), he argues that successful creator programs depend on narrative alignment, audience trust, and long-term partnerships rather than celebrity status or follower counts.

Instead of treating influencer campaigns as isolated promotional activities, companies should integrate creators into broader brand-building and content distribution strategies.

---

### Key Research Insights

#### Narrative matters more than reach

Successful creator partnerships depend on story alignment rather than audience size.

Brands should collaborate with individuals whose expertise naturally supports the company's positioning.

---

#### Influencer marketing is a brand investment

Creator content should be evaluated using metrics such as:

- Brand awareness
- Reach
- Engagement
- Trust
- Community growth

rather than immediate lead generation.

---

#### Repurpose creator content

Dave emphasizes using creator-generated content across:

- LinkedIn
- Newsletters
- Podcasts
- Events
- Sales enablement
- Case studies

One collaboration should generate multiple content assets.

---

#### Give creators creative freedom

Authentic creator content consistently outperforms heavily scripted promotional campaigns.

Companies should provide objectives while allowing creators to communicate in their own voice.

---

#### Commit for the long term

Small, low-commitment experiments rarely produce meaningful brand impact.

Successful creator strategies require consistent investment, patience, and long-term relationship building.

---

### Why It Matters

The newsletter demonstrates that LinkedIn organic growth is strengthened through trusted voices, authentic partnerships, and educational content rather than purely promotional campaigns.

These principles support long-term brand authority within B2B SaaS markets.

---

### Personal Research Notes

This newsletter reinforces Dave's broader marketing philosophy that trust compounds over time. Creator partnerships, authentic storytelling, and educational content consistently outperform transactional marketing because they strengthen relationships before attempting to generate demand.

# Adam Robinson

## Newsletter 1

### Title

Did the King of LinkedIn Go Nuts?

### Published

May 9, 2026

### Author

Adam Robinson

### Source

https://www.adam-robinson.com/

---

## Executive Summary

In this newsletter, Adam Robinson examines Chris Walker's dramatic shift in content strategy after launching his new venture, ENCODED. While many marketers viewed Chris's new content direction as confusing or disconnected from his previous work, Adam argues that the transition reflects a deliberate founder-brand strategy rather than inconsistency. He explains how strong personal brands can successfully pivot into new markets because they have already established trust with their audience.

---

## Why This Matters for LinkedIn Organic Strategy

This newsletter highlights several important lessons about building an influential LinkedIn presence beyond simply publishing content.

Key LinkedIn principles include:

- Build audience trust before launching products.
- Personal brands provide long-term marketing leverage.
- Consistent storytelling strengthens authority.
- Top-of-funnel awareness compounds over time.
- Organic content creates opportunities that paid advertising cannot replicate.
- Authentic opinions and unique narratives differentiate creators.

Rather than focusing on engagement metrics alone, Adam emphasizes that founder-led content should create trust and familiarity long before customers enter the buying journey.

---

## Core Insights

### 1. Founder Trust Is the Greatest Marketing Asset

Chris Walker spent years building credibility through educational marketing before introducing an entirely different product. Adam argues that this accumulated trust allowed Chris to successfully shift into a new category without starting from zero.

---

### 2. Personal Brands Can Reinvent Themselves

Strong creator brands are not limited to one topic forever. Once credibility is established, audiences often follow creators because they trust the individual rather than a specific product.

---

### 3. Organic Content Builds Top-of-Funnel Advantage

Adam highlights that Chris possesses an enormous top-of-funnel advantage because thousands of marketers already consume his content. Every new venture benefits from years of audience development.

---

### 4. Storytelling Creates Stronger Connections

Rather than explaining product features immediately, Adam uses storytelling to describe his own experience with ENCODED before discussing its value proposition. This creates curiosity and emotional engagement before introducing the business.

---

### 5. Authenticity Drives Word-of-Mouth

Adam predicts ENCODED will succeed because customers who genuinely benefit from the product will naturally recommend it. Founder credibility combined with customer advocacy creates sustainable organic growth.

---

↓

↓

↓

↓

↓

Scale Business

---

## Key Takeaways

- Trust compounds over years of consistent content creation.
- Founder brands can successfully enter new markets.
- Storytelling strengthens product positioning.
- Organic content generates long-term awareness.
- Authenticity is more valuable than short-term virality.
- Strong personal brands reduce customer acquisition costs.

---

## Personal Research Notes

This newsletter demonstrates that LinkedIn organic strategy extends beyond posting consistently. Adam shows how founder branding creates durable business advantages by establishing trust that can later be transferred into entirely new products and markets. The newsletter reinforces the importance of long-term audience building rather than short-term promotional campaigns.

---

## Executive Summary

In this newsletter, Adam Robinson presents a repeatable framework for building a B2B SaaS business from zero to one million dollars in annual recurring revenue. Rather than relying on paid acquisition, he explains how founder branding, LinkedIn content, creator partnerships, and AI-powered operations combine to create a scalable organic growth engine.

---

## Why This Matters for LinkedIn Organic Strategy

Although positioned as a startup growth framework, the article is fundamentally about using LinkedIn as a primary customer acquisition channel.

Key LinkedIn principles include:

- Founder branding drives awareness.
- Consistent publishing creates trust.
- Riding industry trends accelerates audience growth.
- Creator collaborations expand organic reach.
- Educational content attracts qualified customers.
- AI improves execution without replacing authentic communication.

The newsletter illustrates how LinkedIn content becomes the foundation for sustainable SaaS growth rather than simply a promotional platform.

---

## Core Insights

### 1. Founder Brand Creates Demand

Adam identifies founder branding as the first growth lever for every startup. By consistently publishing insights, documenting progress, and sharing experiences publicly, founders generate awareness before customers begin evaluating products.

---

### 2. Ride Existing Market Trends

Instead of forcing attention toward a new topic, Adam intentionally aligns his content with rapidly growing conversations around Claude AI. Leveraging existing market momentum increases organic visibility.

---

### 3. Creator-Led Distribution

Adam proposes partnering with creators through live workshops, educational content, and community collaboration. Rather than treating creators as advertisers, he positions them as long-term partners in audience development.

---

### 4. Product Simplicity Improves Marketing

Simple products produce clear messaging. By limiting MoltSets to a small number of core capabilities, Adam ensures that every content asset communicates a consistent value proposition.

---

### 5. AI Enables Scalable Operations

AI automates repetitive customer interactions, documentation, and support, allowing founders to dedicate more time to creating content, engaging with customers, and strengthening their personal brand.
