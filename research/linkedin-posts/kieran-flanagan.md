# Post 2 — The Four AI Marketing Archetypes

**Date Collected:** 03 July 2026

**Source:** LinkedIn

**Theme:**
AI-Native Marketing | Marketing Leadership | Team Design | Organic Growth

**Summary**

Kieran argues that AI is fundamentally changing how marketing organizations should be structured. Rather than maintaining highly specialized marketing departments, future teams will revolve around four high-leverage archetypes that combine creativity, strategic thinking, AI fluency, and human connection.

He explains that as software creation becomes increasingly commoditized through AI, competitive advantage shifts away from execution and toward differentiation through storytelling, taste, experimentation, and community building.

Instead of hiring traditional functional marketers, organizations should build multidisciplinary teams capable of operating alongside AI while focusing on uniquely human strengths.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. The Narrator creates differentiation

As AI lowers the cost of building products, positioning and storytelling become stronger competitive advantages.

Narrators create compelling narratives around products, founders, and customer problems that AI alone cannot generate.

**Observation**

Clear messaging increasingly becomes more valuable than product features.

---

### 2. Taste becomes a strategic advantage

AI can rapidly generate creative assets, but it cannot consistently replicate human judgment.

The Tastemaker applies experience, intuition, and customer understanding to determine what truly resonates with an audience.

Rather than producing more content, successful marketers will produce more distinctive content.

---

### 3. Growth becomes AI-native

Modern growth marketers should combine traditional marketing expertise with AI automation, experimentation, systems thinking, and lightweight engineering skills.

Rather than depending on developers, marketers can increasingly build workflows, automate processes, analyze data, and launch experiments independently.

Growth becomes faster because AI reduces technical barriers.

---

### 4. Human connection becomes increasingly valuable

As digital experiences become more automated, people increasingly value authentic relationships, communities, live events, and meaningful conversations.

Kieran explains that companies should intentionally invest in experiences that AI cannot replicate.

Community becomes a competitive moat.

---

### 5. Marketing teams become multidisciplinary

Rather than organizing around isolated functions like content, product marketing, PR, or demand generation, future organizations will combine complementary skills into highly adaptable marketing teams capable of rapid execution.

This creates greater organizational agility in AI-native environments.

---

## Recurring Marketing Principles

- Storytelling creates differentiation
- Human judgment remains valuable
- AI amplifies execution
- Community builds long-term trust
- Cross-functional marketers outperform specialists

---

## Why This Post Matters

This post demonstrates Kieran's forward-looking perspective on how AI changes marketing talent rather than simply changing marketing tools.

Instead of encouraging marketers to compete with AI, he identifies the uniquely human capabilities that become increasingly valuable as automation expands.

The framework provides practical guidance for marketing leaders redesigning teams for the AI era while maintaining competitive differentiation through creativity, judgment, and relationships.

# Post 3 — Own the Intelligence, Rent the Model

**Date Collected:** 03 July 2026

**Source:** LinkedIn

**Theme:**
AI Strategy | Marketing Intelligence | Knowledge Management | Organic B2B Growth

**Summary**

Kieran argues that a marketing team's greatest competitive advantage is no longer the AI model it uses, but the proprietary intelligence it develops over time.

He explains that every campaign, customer interaction, experiment, and lesson creates valuable marketing knowledge. Organizations that fail to capture this intelligence gradually lose it whenever employees leave or rely entirely on generic AI outputs.

Rather than outsourcing strategic thinking to large language models, Kieran recommends building an internal intelligence layer that compounds over time and can be integrated into any AI system. In this model, AI becomes an execution engine while proprietary marketing knowledge remains the organization's most valuable asset.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Marketing intelligence is the real competitive advantage

The greatest marketing asset is not AI itself but the organization's accumulated understanding of customers, messaging, experiments, and market dynamics.

**Observation**

Knowledge compounds into long-term competitive advantage.

---

### 2. AI models should execute—not replace thinking

AI provides scalable execution, but strategic differentiation comes from proprietary insights collected through years of experience.

Organizations should avoid allowing generic AI outputs to replace original thinking.

---

### 3. Build an internal intelligence layer

Rather than scattering valuable knowledge across documents, conversations, and individual employees, marketing teams should continuously capture lessons, experiments, successful frameworks, and customer insights into a centralized knowledge system.

This allows every AI workflow to operate with organizational context instead of generic internet knowledge.

---

### 4. Experience creates better AI outputs

AI becomes significantly more valuable when combined with proprietary marketing intelligence.

Instead of starting every prompt from scratch, marketers should provide AI with historical context, proven frameworks, customer research, and accumulated organizational knowledge.

---

### 5. Organizational learning compounds over time

Every campaign, experiment, success, and failure should strengthen future decision-making.

Companies that systematically document their marketing intelligence create a compounding advantage that competitors cannot easily replicate.

---

## Recurring Marketing Principles

- Intelligence compounds over time
- AI should enhance—not replace—strategy
- Proprietary knowledge creates differentiation
- Continuous learning strengthens marketing
- Organizational memory creates sustainable advantage

---

## Why This Post Matters

This post represents one of Kieran's core philosophies regarding AI-enabled marketing. Rather than viewing AI models as the primary competitive advantage, he emphasizes that proprietary organizational intelligence is the true long-term asset.

The framework shifts the conversation away from prompt engineering and toward building durable systems that preserve marketing expertise across teams, campaigns, and changing AI technologies.

---

## Personal Research Notes

Across his content, Kieran consistently distinguishes between execution and intelligence. AI dramatically improves execution speed, but sustainable differentiation comes from accumulated customer knowledge, strategic judgment, and proprietary insights that organizations intentionally preserve and continuously refine.
