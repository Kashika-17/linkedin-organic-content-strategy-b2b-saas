# LinkedIn Post 1

## Title

Is It Even Possible to Grow a LinkedIn Following for a Company Account These Days?

### Core Message

Emily analyzed LinkedIn follower data across one hundred high-growth B2B startups to understand how company pages compare with founder-led personal brands.

Instead of relying on assumptions, she gathered first-party data and identified a clear pattern: founder accounts dominate during the early stages of company growth, while company pages begin outperforming founders once businesses reach significant scale.

---

### Core Insights

#### Founder-Led Content Wins Early

Companies with less than $100M in funding typically generate stronger organic reach through founders than through company pages.

Founders build trust faster because audiences connect with individuals before brands.

---

#### Company Pages Scale Later

As organizations mature and brand awareness increases, company pages begin accumulating larger audiences.

Brand equity eventually complements founder influence rather than replacing it.

---

#### Research Creates Authority

Instead of presenting an opinion, Emily supports her conclusions with original research collected across one hundred B2B companies.

This transforms a simple observation into highly credible thought leadership.

---

### Key Takeaways

- Founder branding should be prioritized during early-stage growth.
- Company pages become more valuable as brand awareness increases.
- Data-backed content consistently outperforms unsupported opinions.
- Original research creates stronger credibility and discussion.
