# LinkedIn Post 2

## Title

LinkedIn Is Changing Fast

## Core Message

After attending LinkedIn's private creator event, Devin explains that the platform is evolving from one universal feed into multiple interest-driven discovery feeds.

Success will increasingly depend on expertise rather than broad appeal.

---

## Feature 1 — Collaborative Posts

Creators can publish a shared post together.

Benefits include:

- Combined audiences
- Shared engagement
- Greater organic reach
- Stronger partnership marketing

Rather than hoping another creator reposts your content, reach is built collaboratively from publication.

---

## Feature 2 — Suggested Feeds

LinkedIn is moving toward topic-specific feeds.

Examples include:

- AI at Work
- Economy
- Work Trends

Instead of rewarding generic business advice, LinkedIn increasingly recommends specialized expertise.

---

## Strategic Takeaways

Future winners will:

- Build niche authority
- Publish consistently
- Create educational content
- Develop recognizable expertise

Generic motivational content will gradually lose visibility.

---

## Why This Matters

This post demonstrates Devin's understanding of platform evolution rather than simply reacting to algorithm updates.

His recommendation is clear:

Go deeper—not broader.

# LinkedIn Post 3

## Title

Don't Write Like a Journalist. Write Like Hollywood.

## Core Message

Most B2B marketers try to educate before capturing attention.

Devin argues the opposite.

The first responsibility of content is to make readers feel something.

Only then should you teach.

---

## Traditional B2B Content

"Here's what the data says about discovery calls."

Readers understand the information but rarely remember it.

---

## Story-Driven Content

"You're five minutes into a discovery call.

The buyer says your price is too high.

Your palms are sweating.

What do you do?"

The audience immediately imagines themselves inside the situation.

Only after emotional engagement should education begin.

---

## Lessons from Gong

During his time leading content at Gong, Devin observed that the highest-performing content rarely started with:

- Statistics
- Product features
- Research reports

Instead it started with:

- Customer moments
- Emotional tension
- Familiar experiences

Once readers identified themselves inside the story, the educational lesson became far more memorable.

---

## Core Principle

Emotion first.

Education second.

Trust before promotion.

---

## Why This Matters

This philosophy explains why Gong became one of the strongest content brands in B2B SaaS.

The goal is not simply informing buyers.

The goal is making buyers feel understood.
