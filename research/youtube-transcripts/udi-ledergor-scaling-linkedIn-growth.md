## Why this video was selected

This interview provides one of the clearest behind-the-scenes explanations of how Gong built one of the strongest LinkedIn growth engines in B2B SaaS.

Rather than focusing on individual viral posts or growth hacks, Udi explains how Gong systematically combined employee advocacy, corporate thought leadership, original research, educational content, and founder branding into a repeatable organic marketing system.

The discussion also reveals Gong's internal Content Council, employee enablement strategy, psychological safety culture, and content distribution framework that allowed hundreds of employees to become authentic brand advocates.

These insights directly support the objective of understanding how LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy can become a scalable customer acquisition and brand-building channel for B2B SaaS companies.

---

## Executive Summary

Udi explains that successful LinkedIn marketing is built through systems rather than isolated content.

Instead of relying solely on the corporate page or founder profile, Gong created an ecosystem where executives, marketers, salespeople, customer success teams, and customers all contributed to the company's organic reach.

He argues that employee advocacy succeeds only when employees are encouraged to develop their own authentic voices rather than reposting scripted company messaging.

Throughout the discussion, Udi demonstrates how valuable educational content, psychological safety, employee empowerment, original research, and consistent community engagement combine into one of the strongest organic LinkedIn growth engines in B2B SaaS.

## Core Research Observations

### Employee advocacy scales organic reach

Gong treats employees as creators rather than content distributors.

Instead of forcing employees to copy company messaging, individuals are encouraged to create their own perspectives while supporting common company themes.

---

### Authenticity outperforms scripted messaging

People trust authentic employee voices significantly more than polished corporate communications.

Employees are encouraged to personalize company content using their own experiences and writing style.

---

### Corporate pages and personal brands should grow together

Udi explains that successful LinkedIn strategies do not choose between founder branding and company branding.

Instead, both should reinforce one another and grow simultaneously.

---

### Internal creators become long-term marketing assets

Rather than hiring external influencers, Gong developed internal employees into respected industry creators.

This strengthened both personal brands and the company's overall authority.

---

### Community compounds distribution

Organic reach increases when employees, customers, executives, and partners naturally participate in conversations around company content.

Community-driven engagement consistently outperforms artificial engagement tactics.

---

### Psychological safety enables better content

Employees create stronger content when they feel safe experimenting, sharing opinions, and occasionally failing.

Creative cultures consistently outperform approval-heavy organizations.

---

### Valuable content earns amplification

The highest-performing LinkedIn posts solve real problems, teach practical lessons, or introduce original insights.

People willingly share content that improves their own professional reputation.

## LinkedIn Lessons

Udi demonstrates that LinkedIn growth is the result of coordinated value creation rather than algorithm optimization.

Successful companies use LinkedIn to:

- Educate their audience
- Build employee brands
- Encourage authentic conversations
- Publish original research
- Empower internal creators
- Strengthen customer relationships
- Build long-term trust

Rather than chasing viral moments, LinkedIn becomes a continuous ecosystem of education, credibility, and community engagement.
