## Why this video was selected

This interview provides one of the strongest explanations of how Gong built one of the most recognizable B2B SaaS brands almost entirely through organic marketing.

Instead of relying on paid acquisition, Udi explains how Gong invested in educational content, proprietary research, category creation, and thought leadership to become an authority within the revenue intelligence market.

The discussion goes beyond LinkedIn posting tactics and explains how long-term trust, customer education, unique industry insights, and courageous marketing create sustainable competitive advantages.

The insights directly support the objective of understanding how LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy can become a scalable customer acquisition engine for B2B SaaS companies.

---

## Executive Summary

Udi explains that great B2B marketing is not about publishing more content—it is about publishing content that people genuinely want to consume.

Throughout Gong's growth, the marketing team focused on creating educational resources, proprietary research, customer stories, and category-defining insights instead of promotional campaigns. This approach helped Gong build trust long before prospects entered the buying cycle.

He argues that marketers should stop copying industry "best practices" because once something becomes a best practice, it is already ordinary. Instead, companies should develop courageous marketing that introduces original perspectives, teaches customers, challenges conventional thinking, and builds authority through consistency.

The interview demonstrates how LinkedIn, content marketing, employee advocacy, proprietary research, and category creation work together as a single long-term organic growth system.

## Core Research Observations

### Best practices create average marketing

Udi argues that marketers who copy "best practices" inevitably create work that looks identical to everyone else's.

Instead of following trends, companies should develop distinctive positioning, bold ideas, and original perspectives that make their brand memorable.

---

### Educational content outperforms promotional content

People follow brands that consistently teach valuable lessons.

Rather than promoting products directly, Gong focuses on helping sales leaders improve performance through practical insights, research, and educational content.

---

### Organic marketing compounds over time

Consistent educational publishing creates long-term brand equity.

Every article, report, podcast, LinkedIn post, and research publication strengthens audience trust while continuously expanding organic reach.

---

### Original research creates authority

Companies should leverage proprietary customer data whenever possible.

Exclusive research cannot easily be replicated by competitors and naturally positions the company as an industry authority.

---

### Category creation begins with education

Customers must first understand the problem before they appreciate the solution.

Gong invested years educating the market about Revenue Intelligence before the category became widely recognized.

---

### Trust is built before buyers enter the market

Most potential customers are not actively buying today.

Educational content creates positive memory structures that position the brand as the first choice when purchase decisions eventually occur.

---

### Great content earns distribution

Instead of paying for visibility, Gong creates content valuable enough that customers, media outlets, and creators willingly share it.

Earned distribution consistently outperforms purely paid distribution over the long term.
