# Video 2

## Why Going Viral Is Bad B2B Marketing

**Source**
G2 Industry Insights

### Core Message

Effective B2B content is not measured by how many people see it.

It is measured by whether the right buyers trust it.

Popularity does not equal business impact.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Optimize for Resonance, Not Reach

Many marketers chase impressions.

Devin argues marketers should instead ask:

"Did the right people see this content?"

A post reaching 2,000 qualified buyers is more valuable than one reaching 200,000 irrelevant viewers.

---

### 2. Meaningful Engagement Beats Vanity Metrics

Rather than focusing on likes or impressions, marketers should prioritize:

- Saves
- Shares
- Meaningful comments
- Conversations
- Buyer engagement

These metrics indicate trust and long-term influence.

---

### 3. Balance Evergreen and Trending Content

Devin recommends combining two content types.

#### Evergreen Content

Topics that remain useful over time:

- Frameworks
- Playbooks
- Lessons
- Stories
- Processes

---

#### Trending Content

Content responding to:

- Industry news
- Product launches
- AI developments
- Market shifts

Evergreen content builds authority, while timely content demonstrates relevance.

---

### 4. Measure Leading Indicators

Content marketing ROI develops over time.

Instead of expecting immediate revenue, marketers should monitor:

Early indicators

- Views
- Engagement
- Follower growth

Later indicators

- Website traffic
- Demo requests
- Pipeline
- Revenue

---

### 5. Build With Your Audience

Instead of broadcasting content, invite participation.

Examples include:

- Polls
- Newsletter contributions
- Community discussions
- Audience questions
- Product feedback

People are more invested in content they help shape.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Prioritize relevance over reach.
- Focus on buyer trust instead of viral metrics.
- Mix evergreen and timely content.
- Track early engagement before revenue.
- Turn audiences into active communities.

Rather than discussing abstract marketing theory, every recommendation is tied to practical execution and measurable business outcomes.

# Strategic Principles

Across both videos, Devin's LinkedIn philosophy can be summarized into six principles:

1. Build trust before trying to sell.
2. Teach practical lessons instead of promoting products.
3. Share proof and real experiences to establish credibility.
4. Create content for your ideal buyers, not for everyone.
5. Stay consistent and allow reputation to compound.
6. Treat LinkedIn as a long-term business asset rather than a short-term marketing channel.
