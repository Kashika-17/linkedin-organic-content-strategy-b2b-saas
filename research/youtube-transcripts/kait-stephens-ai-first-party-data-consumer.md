## Executive Summary

In this presentation, Kait Stephens explores how AI is transforming customer discovery, purchase behavior, and omnichannel marketing.

Using original consumer research, she explains that shoppers increasingly rely on AI assistants throughout the buying journey. As AI platforms become trusted recommendation engines, competitive advantage shifts away from advertising budgets and toward first-party data quality, customer reviews, and structured product information.

Kait argues that brands must prepare for AI-first commerce by building strong customer data infrastructure, integrating offline and online purchase signals, and optimizing content for large language models rather than traditional search engines alone.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. AI Has Become Part of the Buying Journey

Consumers increasingly use AI to:

- Discover products
- Compare alternatives
- Evaluate trust
- Validate purchasing decisions

AI now influences buying behavior long before customers visit brand websites.

---

### 2. Trust Comes from Customer Signals

LLMs prioritize:

- Customer reviews
- Community discussions
- Reddit conversations
- Product experiences

Authentic customer feedback becomes a critical marketing asset.

---

### 3. First-Party Data is the Competitive Advantage

Brands that connect:

- Retail purchases
- Customer identities
- Product usage
- Marketing systems

will outperform brands relying solely on third-party advertising data.

---

### 4. Omnichannel Attribution Requires Better Infrastructure

Offline purchases should feed digital marketing systems to improve attribution, personalization, and future campaign performance.

---

### 5. AI Optimization Requires Better Content

Rather than optimizing only for search engines, brands must create structured, trustworthy, and context-rich content that AI systems can accurately understand and recommend.

This presentation demonstrates how Kait's marketing philosophy is evolving for the AI era.
